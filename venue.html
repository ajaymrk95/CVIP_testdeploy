Conference Venue

Host Institute: NIT Calicut

National Institute of Technology Calicut (NITC) is a Technical Institution of National Importance. The campus is located about 22 km north-east of Calicut City in the picturesque foothills of the Western Ghats. It is renowned for its academic excellence and lush, green landscape.

Historical Significance of Kozhikode

Kozhikode (Calicut) holds a pivotal place in world history as the landing site of the Portuguese explorer Vasco da Gama in 1498. This event marked the establishment of the sea route from Europe to India, fundamentally changing global trade.

Known historically as the "City of Spices," Calicut was a major trading hub for black pepper and cardamom during the Middle Ages and continues to be a vibrant cultural and culinary center in Kerala today.
